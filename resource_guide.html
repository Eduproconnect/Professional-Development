Resource Guide: Building Relationships - The Heart of Effective Teaching

Books for Professional Development

On Teacher-Student Relationships

  • "Because I Had a Teacher" by Kobi Yamada
  • A heartwarming picture book celebrating the impact teachers have on students' lives

  • Excellent for reflection and inspiration

  • "The Courage to Teach" by Parker J. Palmer

  • Explores the inner landscape of teaching and the connection between identity and teaching practice

  • Focuses on teaching from a place of authenticity and integrity

  • "Relationship-Driven Classroom Management" by John M. Vitto

  • Practical strategies for building positive relationships with challenging students
  • Research-based approaches to creating a supportive classroom community

On Student Engagement and Motivation

  • "Grit: The Power of Passion and Perseverance" by Angela Duckworth
  • Research on how perseverance and passion contribute to long-term success

  • Strategies for developing grit in students and ourselves

  • "Drive: The Surprising Truth About What Motivates Us" by Daniel Pink

  • Explores intrinsic motivation and its connection to relationships and autonomy
  • Practical applications for the classroom

On Cultural Responsiveness

  • "Culturally Responsive Teaching and the Brain" by Zaretta Hammond
  • Connects neuroscience research to culturally responsive teaching practices

  • Strategies for building trust with culturally diverse students

  • "We Want to Do More Than Survive" by Bettina Love

  • Explores educational justice and the importance of seeing students' full humanity
  • Challenges educators to move beyond surface-level cultural responsiveness

Films for Professional Learning

Teacher-Student Relationships

  • Key scenes: Journal writing introduction, "Line Game" activity, Holocaust survivor visit

  • Themes: Creating safe spaces for authentic expression, connecting curriculum to students' lives

  • The Ron Clark Story (2006)

  • Key scenes: Classroom rules introduction, home visits, academic competitions

  • Themes: High expectations with strong support, teacher persistence and dedication

  • Beyond the Blackboard (2011)

  • Key scenes: First day at the shelter school, classroom transformation, student celebrations
  • Themes: Seeing potential beyond circumstances, creating dignity in challenging environments

Engaging Diverse Students

  • Dangerous Minds (1995)
  • Key scenes: Poetry introduction, candy bar incentives, Dylan Thomas lesson

  • Themes: Finding relevant entry points, adapting curriculum to student interests

  • The Great Debaters (2007)

  • Key scenes: Debate team formation, "What is your source?" lesson, first competition

  • Themes: Empowering student voice, academic excellence as resistance

  • McFarland, USA (2015)

  • Key scenes: Home visits, early morning practices, cultural misunderstandings
  • Themes: Understanding cultural context, building on student strengths

Teacher Growth and Challenges

  • Half Nelson (2006)
  • Key scenes: Dialectics lesson, student discovering teacher's addiction, final scene

  • Themes: Teacher vulnerability, complex teacher-student relationships, ethical boundaries

  • Dead Poets Society (1989)

  • Key scenes: "O Captain, My Captain," ripping out textbook pages, final standing on desks
  • Themes: Teaching authenticity, inspiring passion for learning, challenging conventions

Assessment Tools

Relationship Assessment

  • Student-Teacher Relationship Scale (STRS)
  • Validated tool for assessing teacher-student relationship quality

  • Measures closeness, conflict, and dependency

  • Classroom Assessment Scoring System (CLASS)

  • Observation tool that includes emotional support and classroom organization domains
  • Provides framework for improving teacher-student interactions

Classroom Climate Assessment

  • Comprehensive School Climate Inventory (CSCI)
  • Measures perceptions of school climate, including relationships

  • Available from the National School Climate Center

  • My Class Inventory (MCI)

  • Simple tool for assessing classroom environment
  • Measures satisfaction, friction, competitiveness, difficulty, and cohesiveness

Implementation Support

Peer Observation Protocol

  • Focus on teacher-student interactions
  • Look for evidence of relationship-building strategies
  • Document positive interactions and areas for growth
  • Provide specific, constructive feedback

Professional Learning Community Discussion Guide

  • Monthly focus topics based on relationship-building principles
  • Structured protocol for sharing successes and challenges
  • Collaborative problem-solving for relationship difficulties
  • Resource sharing and strategy development

Student Feedback Templates

  • Age-appropriate surveys for student perceptions of classroom relationships
  • Reflection prompts for students to provide feedback on classroom climate
  • Exit ticket templates focused on belonging and connection
  • End-of-unit relationship assessment questions

Remember

Building strong teacher-student relationships is both an art and a science. These resources provide research, inspiration, and practical strategies, but the most important ingredient is your authentic commitment to connecting with your students as unique individuals with valuable perspectives, experiences, and potential.
