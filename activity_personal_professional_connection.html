Activity Handout: Personal-Professional Connection

Purpose

To explore how to authentically integrate your personal passions and strengths into your teaching to build stronger connections with students.

Time Required

30 minutes

Materials Needed

  • This handout
  • Pen or pencil
  • Optional: colored markers or pencils

Instructions

Part 1: Personal Inventory (10 minutes)

Reflect on and answer the following questions:

  1. Personal Passions: What topics, activities, or ideas are you genuinely passionate about outside of teaching?
  1. Personal Strengths: What are your natural talents or strengths? What do others say you're good at?
  1. Meaningful Experiences: What life experiences have shaped who you are and how you see the world?
  1. Values: What core values guide your life and work? What matters most to you?
  1. Interests: What topics do you enjoy learning about or discussing in your free time?

Part 2: Classroom Connection (10 minutes)

For each area you identified above, brainstorm how you might authentically integrate these elements into your teaching:

  1. Sharing Appropriately: What aspects of yourself could you appropriately share with students to build connection?
  1. Curriculum Connections: How might your interests or experiences enhance curriculum content?
  1. Teaching Approach: How do your personal strengths influence your teaching style?
  1. Classroom Environment: How might your values shape your classroom culture?
  1. Student Engagement: How could your passions spark student interest or engagement?

Part 3: Implementation Plan (10 minutes)

Choose 2-3 specific ways you will integrate your authentic self into your teaching. For each one:

  1. Specific Action: What exactly will you do?
  1. When/How Often: When will you implement this? How frequently?
  1. Purpose: How will this build connection with students?
  1. Boundaries: What appropriate boundaries will you maintain?

Examples to Consider

Appropriate Personal Sharing

  • Sharing relevant stories from your own educational journey
  • Discussing books you're currently reading related to curriculum
  • Talking about how you use subject matter in your own life
  • Sharing appropriate photos of your pets, travels, or hobbies
  • Discussing your own learning process and challenges

Curriculum Integration

  • A history teacher who loves cooking might incorporate historical recipes
  • A math teacher who enjoys sports might use sports statistics in problems
  • A language arts teacher who writes poetry might share their own writing process
  • A science teacher who gardens might bring in plant examples
  • A music teacher who plays in a band might share recordings of performances

Teaching Approach

  • A teacher who values collaboration might emphasize group projects
  • A teacher who is naturally organized might create clear visual systems
  • A teacher who is artistic might incorporate more visual elements
  • A teacher who loves storytelling might use narrative approaches
  • A teacher who is analytical might emphasize critical thinking

Reflection Questions

  • How might sharing appropriate aspects of yourself enhance student engagement?
  • What boundaries are important to maintain while being authentic?
  • How might your authenticity model important qualities for students?
  • What concerns do you have about bringing more of yourself into your teaching?

Remember

Authentic teaching doesn't mean sharing everything about yourself or becoming friends with students. It means teaching from who you are, not just what you know, while maintaining appropriate professional boundaries.
