Activity Handout: Classroom Climate Audit

Purpose

To assess your current classroom climate and identify specific changes to create a more safe, hospitable learning environment.

Time Required

30 minutes

Materials Needed

  • This handout
  • Pen or pencil
  • Sticky notes for action planning

Instructions

Part 1: Self-Assessment (15 minutes)

Rate each aspect of your classroom climate on a scale of 1-5: 1 = Needs significant improvement 2 = Needs some improvement 3 = Adequate 4 = Strong 5 = Exemplary

For each item, note specific evidence and ideas for improvement.

Part 2: Action Planning (10 minutes)

  1. Identify 3-5 priority areas for improvement based on your self-assessment.
  2. For each priority area, write one specific, actionable step you will take on a sticky note.
  3. Arrange your sticky notes in order of implementation (what you'll do first, second, etc.).

Part 3: Partner Discussion (5 minutes)

Share your top priorities and action steps with a partner. Provide feedback and suggestions to each other.

Classroom Climate Audit Tool

Physical Environment

Aspect Rating Evidence Ideas for Improvement
The physical arrangement of the room facilitates interaction and community
The classroom displays reflect student diversity and contributions
There are comfortable spaces for different learning activities
The classroom feels welcoming upon entry
Materials and resources are accessible to all students

Routines and Procedures

Aspect Rating Evidence Ideas for Improvement
Daily routines create predictability and security
Transitions are smooth and efficient
Procedures emphasize community and mutual support
Students know what to do when they need help
Routines exist for celebrating student successes

Teacher Language and Interactions

Aspect Rating Evidence Ideas for Improvement
I greet each student personally each day
I use positive, affirming language consistently
I show genuine interest in students' lives
I respond to mistakes with support rather than criticism
I balance high expectations with emotional support

Student-to-Student Interactions

Aspect Rating Evidence Ideas for Improvement
Students treat each other with respect
Students collaborate effectively
Students celebrate each other's successes
Students support each other during challenges
Conflicts are resolved constructively

Cultural Responsiveness

Aspect Rating Evidence Ideas for Improvement
Curriculum includes diverse perspectives and voices
Student cultural backgrounds are valued and incorporated
I recognize and address bias or stereotypes
I adapt instruction to diverse learning styles
I communicate effectively with all families

Emotional Safety

Aspect Rating Evidence Ideas for Improvement
Students feel comfortable sharing ideas and questions
Risk-taking and mistakes are normalized as part of learning
Students' emotional needs are recognized and addressed
There are protocols for handling sensitive topics
Students have strategies for managing emotions

Reflection Questions

  • What patterns do you notice in your self-assessment?
  • What strengths can you build upon?
  • What quick wins could you implement immediately?
  • What longer-term changes would have the most impact?
  • What resources or support do you need to improve your classroom climate?

Remember

Creating a safe, hospitable learning environment is an ongoing process, not a one-time event. Small, consistent changes often have more impact than dramatic overhauls. Focus on progress, not perfection.
