Professional Development Session Outline

Building Relationships: The Heart of Effective Teaching

Session Overview

Title: Building Relationships: The Heart of Effective Teaching
Duration: Full-day workshop (6 hours with breaks)
Target Audience: K-12 teachers of all experience levels
Purpose: To explore the critical role of teacher-student relationships in effective teaching and provide practical strategies for building and maintaining these relationships

Session Goals

By the end of this professional development session, participants will: 1. Understand the research-based connection between teacher-student relationships and student success 2. Reflect on their own relationship-building practices and identify areas for growth 3. Learn practical strategies for building authentic connections with all students 4. Develop approaches for creating safe, hospitable learning environments 5. Explore methods for balancing high expectations with strong support 6. Create an action plan for implementing relationship-building strategies in their classrooms

Materials Needed

  • Copies of "Cause I Ain't Got a Pencil" poem by Joshua T. Dickerson
  • Excerpts from "Because I Had a Teacher" by Kobi Yamada
  • Video clips from:
  • Dead Poets Society
  • Rita Pierson's TED Talk "Every Kid Needs a Champion"
  • Selected film clips from Freedom Writers, The Ron Clark Story, etc.
  • Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie's TED Talk "The Danger of a Single Story"
  • Handouts with key strategies and reflection questions
  • Chart paper and markers for group activities
  • Sticky notes for individual reflections
  • Journals/notebooks for personal action planning

Session Schedule

8:30-9:00 AM: Welcome and Introduction (30 minutes)

  • Welcome and session overview
  • Opening activity: "Teacher Who Made a Difference"
  • Participants reflect on and share about a teacher who made a positive impact on their lives
  • Identify what specific actions or qualities made that teacher memorable
  • Introduction of the day's key themes and agenda

9:00-10:00 AM: The Foundation: Why Relationships Matter (60 minutes)

  • Video: Rita Pierson's TED Talk "Every Kid Needs a Champion"
  • Research overview: The connection between teacher-student relationships and academic outcomes
  • Discussion of Parker Palmer's insights from Dead Poets Society analysis
  • Activity: "Relationship Roadblocks"
  • Small groups identify common barriers to building relationships with students
  • Share strategies for overcoming these barriers
  • Presentation: The 10 key principles of relationship-centered teaching (based on research synthesis)

10:00-10:15 AM: Break (15 minutes)

10:15-11:15 AM: Seeing the Whole Child (60 minutes)

  • Reading and discussion: "Cause I Ain't Got a Pencil" by Joshua T. Dickerson
  • Video clip: Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie's "The Danger of a Single Story"
  • Activity: "Beyond the Surface"
  • Case studies of students with challenging behaviors or circumstances
  • Small groups discuss what might be happening beneath the surface
  • Develop strategies for learning more about students' contexts and strengths
  • Presentation: Strategies for understanding student context without making assumptions
  • Discussion: Balancing empathy with high expectations

11:15 AM-12:15 PM: Creating Safe, Hospitable Learning Spaces (60 minutes)

  • Video clips: Selected scenes from Freedom Writers and The Ron Clark Story
  • Presentation: Elements of a safe, hospitable classroom environment
  • Activity: "Classroom Climate Audit"
  • Self-assessment of current classroom climate
  • Identifying areas for improvement
  • Planning concrete changes to physical space, routines, and interactions
  • Discussion: How to establish trust and safety from day one
  • Reading: Excerpts from "Because I Had a Teacher" focusing on creating welcoming environments

12:15-1:00 PM: Lunch Break (45 minutes)

1:00-2:00 PM: Authentic Teaching and Connection (60 minutes)

  • Video clip: Selected scene from Dead Poets Society showing authentic teaching
  • Discussion: Teaching from who you are, not just what you know
  • Activity: "Personal-Professional Connection"
  • Identifying personal passions and strengths
  • Exploring how to authentically integrate these into teaching
  • Planning ways to share appropriate aspects of self with students
  • Presentation: Boundaries and balance in authentic teacher-student relationships
  • Reflection: "What do I want my students to remember about me in 20 years?"

2:00-3:00 PM: Strategies for Building Relationships with All Students (60 minutes)

  • Video clips: Selected scenes from various films showing relationship-building strategies
  • Presentation: Research-based strategies for connecting with diverse students
  • Activity: "Relationship Toolkit"
  • Small groups explore different relationship-building strategies
  • Create visual representations of key approaches
  • Gallery walk to share and learn from other groups
  • Discussion: Adapting strategies for different age groups and contexts
  • Presentation: Angela Duckworth's research on grit and how relationships support resilience

3:00-3:15 PM: Break (15 minutes)

3:15-4:15 PM: From Theory to Practice: Implementation Planning (60 minutes)

  • Activity: "Monday Morning Plan"
  • Individual reflection on immediate actions to improve relationships
  • Pair-share of concrete implementation ideas
  • Small group feedback and refinement
  • Presentation: Overcoming obstacles to relationship-building
  • Discussion: Balancing relationship-building with curriculum demands
  • Activity: "Commitment and Support"
  • Writing personal commitments to relationship-building
  • Identifying needed resources and support
  • Planning for accountability and follow-through

4:15-4:30 PM: Closing and Evaluation (15 minutes)

  • Final reflection: "One thing I'm taking away from today..."
  • Distribution of additional resources for continued learning
  • Session evaluation
  • Closing inspirational video clip or reading

Follow-Up Activities

  • Peer observation focused on relationship-building strategies
  • Professional learning community discussions using provided discussion guides
  • Mid-year reflection session to share successes and challenges
  • Resource sharing through online platform or email group
  • Individual coaching available for teachers wanting additional support

Assessment of Impact

  • Pre and post self-assessment of relationship-building practices
  • Student feedback surveys (templates provided)
  • Observation protocol focused on teacher-student interactions
  • Tracking of relevant student outcome data (attendance, engagement, achievement)
  • Teacher reflection journals with guided prompts

Additional Resources

  • Annotated bibliography of books, articles, and videos on teacher-student relationships
  • Handout with key quotes and insights from all analyzed sources
  • Templates for relationship-building activities with students
  • Quick-reference guide to the 10 key principles
  • List of film recommendations for further exploration of themes
