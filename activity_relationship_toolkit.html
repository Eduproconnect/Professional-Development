Activity Handout: Relationship Toolkit

Purpose

To explore and share a variety of practical relationship-building strategies that can be implemented in different classroom contexts.

Time Required

45 minutes

Materials Needed

  • This handout
  • Chart paper
  • Markers
  • Sticky notes
  • Optional: digital devices for research

Instructions

Part 1: Strategy Exploration (20 minutes)

  1. Your group has been assigned one of the following relationship-building categories:
  2. Group 1: Beginning of year activities
  3. Group 2: Daily/weekly routines
  4. Group 3: Strategies for challenging students
  5. Group 4: Whole-class community building

  6. Group 5: Curriculum connections

  7. As a group, brainstorm specific strategies within your category. Consider:

  8. Strategies you've used successfully
  9. Approaches you've observed from colleagues
  10. Ideas from today's professional development

  11. Research-based practices

  12. For each strategy, discuss:

  13. How it works (specific steps)
  14. Why it works (research or rationale)
  15. When to use it (context and timing)

  16. Adaptations for different age groups

  17. Create a visual representation of your strategies on chart paper. Be creative!

Part 2: Gallery Walk (15 minutes)

  1. Post your chart paper on the wall.
  2. Half of your group will stay with your poster to explain your strategies.
  3. The other half will rotate to learn about strategies from other groups.
  4. Use sticky notes to add questions or additional ideas to each poster.
  5. Switch roles halfway through so everyone has a chance to learn from other groups.

Part 3: Personal Selection (10 minutes)

  1. After viewing all strategy categories, select:
  2. 1 strategy to implement tomorrow
  3. 2 strategies to implement next week
  4. 1 strategy that will require more planning
  5. Complete the implementation planning section of this handout.

Relationship-Building Strategies Reference

Beginning of Year Activities

  • Student interest inventories
  • Two truths and a lie (teacher version)
  • "Who am I?" puzzle pieces
  • Class values collaborative poster
  • Parent/family communication plan
  • Student success interviews

Daily/Weekly Routines

  • Greeting students at the door
  • Morning meeting or circle time
  • Appreciation or gratitude practices
  • Exit tickets with personal connection
  • Weekly check-ins with individual students
  • Positive notes or phone calls home

Strategies for Challenging Students

  • 2x10 Strategy (2 minutes for 10 days)
  • Strength spotting
  • Special jobs or responsibilities
  • Behavior-specific praise
  • Restorative conversations
  • "Fresh start" opportunities

Whole-Class Community Building

  • Class meetings
  • Collaborative problem-solving
  • Celebration rituals
  • Class challenges or goals
  • Service learning projects
  • Collaborative classroom design

Curriculum Connections

  • Identity-based projects
  • Student choice in assignments
  • Real-world application activities
  • Cultural relevance strategies
  • Student expertise showcases
  • Connecting content to student interests

Reflection Questions

  • Which strategies align best with your teaching style and context?
  • What barriers might you face in implementing these strategies?
  • How might you adapt these strategies for your specific students?
  • What support do you need to successfully implement these approaches?

Remember

Relationship-building is not a one-size-fits-all process. The most effective approaches are those that authentically align with your teaching style and meet the specific needs of your students. Start small, be consistent, and build your relationship toolkit over time.
