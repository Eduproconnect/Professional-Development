Why Relationships Matter

  • "Kids don't learn from people they don't like" - Rita Pierson
  • Research shows:
  • Positive teacher-student relationships predict higher academic achievement
  • Students with strong teacher connections have fewer behavioral problems
  • Relationship quality affects student engagement and motivation
  • The impact is strongest for students with learning or behavioral challenges
  • "Real learning does not happen until students are brought into relationship with the teacher, with each other" - Parker Palmer

10 Key Principles of Relationship-Centered Teaching

  1. Authentic teacher-student relationships form the foundation of learning
  2. Seeing the whole child: context, strengths, and challenges
  3. Creating safe, hospitable learning spaces
  4. Balancing high expectations with strong support
  5. Making learning relevant and engaging
  6. Teacher authenticity and passion
  7. Understanding diverse perspectives and experiences
  8. Teaching as a transformative calling
  9. Fostering resilience and growth mindset
  10. Advocacy and system change

Seeing the Whole Child

  • "The single story creates stereotypes... the problem with stereotypes is not that they are untrue, but that they are incomplete" - Chimamanda Adichie
  • Understanding student context without making assumptions
  • Recognizing strengths and potential even in challenging circumstances
  • Avoiding deficit thinking while acknowledging real challenges
  • Balancing empathy with high expectations

Strategies for Building Relationships with All Students

  • Research-based approaches:
  • Positive greetings at the door
  • 2x10 strategy (2 minutes for 10 days)
  • Interest inventories and student surveys
  • Attending extracurricular events
  • Positive phone calls/notes home
  • Class meetings and circles
  • Personalized feedback
  • "Banking time" with challenging students
