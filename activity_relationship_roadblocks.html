Activity Handout: Relationship Roadblocks

Purpose

To identify common barriers to building relationships with students and develop strategies for overcoming these challenges.

Time Required

30 minutes

Materials Needed

  • Chart paper
  • Markers
  • Sticky notes
  • This handout

Instructions

Part 1: Identifying Roadblocks (10 minutes)

  1. In your small group, discuss the following questions:
  2. What barriers make it difficult to build relationships with students?
  3. What factors make some student relationships more challenging than others?

  4. What systemic obstacles exist in schools that hinder connection?

  5. On your chart paper, create a visual representation of these roadblocks. You might use:

  6. A road with obstacles
  7. A bridge with missing pieces
  8. A wall with barriers

  9. Any other visual metaphor that works for your group

  10. Label each roadblock clearly on your visual.

Part 2: Developing Solutions (10 minutes)

  1. For each roadblock identified, discuss potential solutions or strategies to overcome it.
  2. Write these solutions on sticky notes and place them next to the corresponding roadblocks.
  3. Consider:
  4. What has worked for you in the past?
  5. What resources or support would help?
  6. What mindset shifts might be necessary?
  7. What systemic changes might be needed?

Part 3: Sharing and Synthesis (10 minutes)

  1. Each group will briefly share their visual and key insights with the whole group.
  2. As you listen to other groups, note strategies you want to try.
  3. We'll create a master list of roadblocks and strategies for everyone to reference.

Common Relationship Roadblocks to Consider

Time Constraints

  • Large class sizes
  • Packed curriculum demands
  • Limited non-instructional time
  • Administrative responsibilities

Student Factors

  • Students with challenging behaviors
  • Cultural or language differences
  • Students with trauma backgrounds
  • Students who resist connection

Teacher Factors

  • Fear of crossing professional boundaries
  • Burnout or compassion fatigue
  • Implicit biases or assumptions
  • Limited training in relationship-building

Systemic Factors

  • School policies that prioritize compliance over connection
  • Emphasis on test scores over social-emotional learning
  • Lack of support for relationship-building efforts
  • Physical environment constraints

Reflection Questions

  • Which roadblocks resonate most with your experience?
  • What new strategies did you learn from your colleagues?
  • What one roadblock will you focus on addressing first?
  • What support do you need to overcome your biggest roadblocks?

Remember: Identifying roadblocks is not about making excuses but about recognizing challenges so we can develop effective strategies to overcome them.
